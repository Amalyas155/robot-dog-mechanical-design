## 2. Body Structure

The robot has a rectangular body that provides a stable base for mounting the electronic components, battery, controller, and motors. The design is lightweight, simple, and suitable for a basic quadruped robot.

---

## 3. Leg Design

The robot has four identical legs. Each leg is connected to the body with two rotational joints, allowing forward and backward movement for walking.

---

## 4. Number of Joints

- Four legs
- Two joints per leg
- **Total joints: 8**

---

## 5. Degrees of Freedom (DOF)

Each joint provides one rotational degree of freedom.

**Total DOF = 8**

---

## 6. Preliminary Torque Calculation

### Assumptions
- Robot weight = **3 kg**
- The weight is equally distributed across the four legs.

### Calculation
- Weight supported by each leg = **3 ÷ 4 = 0.75 kg**

### Estimated Torque
A servo motor with approximately **2–3 kg·cm** of torque is sufficient for this preliminary prototype design.

---

## 7. Stability and Center of Gravity

The center of gravity is positioned near the middle of the robot body to improve balance and reduce the possibility of tipping while walking.

---

## 8. Proposed Walking Method

The robot uses a simple diagonal walking gait, where opposite legs move together to provide better balance and stability during movement.

---

## 9. Mechanical Challenges

- Maintaining balance while walking.
- Reducing the overall robot weight.
- Selecting servo motors with sufficient torque.
- Designing strong and durable joints.
